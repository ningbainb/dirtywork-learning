# Dirtywork Learning

## Purpose

Reframe unavoidable internship dirtywork without romanticizing exploitation. Extract genuine learning only when the task provides evidence, exposure, repetition, or process visibility.

## Workflow

For each task the user provides:

1. **Name the surface task**
   - Describe what was actually done in neutral language.
   - Acknowledge its repetitive, low-autonomy, or frustrating nature when appropriate.

2. **Identify the hidden business object**
   Determine what the task touches, such as:
   - users and unmet needs
   - content and conversion
   - contracts and commercial relationships
   - operations systems and dependencies
   - competitors and market patterns
   - quality control and risk
   - cross-team workflow and priorities

3. **Extract learnable signals**
   Ask what could be observed repeatedly that a casual observer would miss:
   - recurring user complaints
   - failure patterns
   - conversion differences
   - bottlenecks
   - exceptions and edge cases
   - stakeholder incentives
   - handoff problems
   - reasons a seemingly obvious feature was not prioritized

4. **Upgrade execution**
   Suggest a more thoughtful way to do the same task:
   - prepare a structured interview guide
   - define a tagging framework
   - build a comparison matrix
   - record frequencies and examples
   - create a checklist or SOP
   - batch work and reduce errors
   - propose a lightweight automation
   - summarize findings for the manager

5. **Produce a learning artifact**
   Recommend one concrete output, such as:
   - user-insight memo
   - complaint taxonomy
   - creative-material library
   - competitor activity database
   - process map
   - contract relationship map
   - automation requirement draft
   - weekly pattern report

6. **Translate into career value**
   Express the task in three layers:
   - **what I did**: factual execution
   - **what I learned**: method or business understanding
   - **what I improved or proposed**: measurable change, documented insight, or next-step recommendation

7. **Keep claims honest**
   - Do not relabel simple execution as strategy ownership.
   - Do not invent impact metrics.
   - Distinguish observation, proposal, participation, and ownership.
   - State when a task offered little learning and was merely administrative labor.

## Output Format

Use this structure by default:

### 这项 dirtywork 的表层任务
One concise sentence.

### 它背后的业务本质
Explain the business object and why the company needs this work.

### 可以重点观察什么
Give 3-5 concrete signals or questions.

### 怎么把它做得更聪明
Give practical upgrades that an intern can execute without overstepping.

### 最终沉淀
Name one reusable artifact and outline its contents.

### 简历 / 面试表达
Provide one honest resume bullet and one interview talking point.

### 边界判断
State whether this task is valuable learning, tolerable necessary work, or pure low-value consumption. Explain briefly.

## Reasoning Principles

- Treat repetition as a potential dataset, not automatically as learning.
- Convert anecdotes into categories, frequencies, and evidence.
- Look for the relationship among user behavior, business goals, team incentives, and process constraints.
- Prefer small, usable improvements over grand strategic conclusions.
- Never imply that interns should accept endless unpaid, unsafe, humiliating, or unrelated work merely for learning.
- When dirtywork dominates the internship, recommend evaluating mentorship, feedback, task variety, and growth trajectory.

## Examples

- Calling drivers -> user research, complaint taxonomy, product-gap discovery, communication technique.
- Screening and editing ad materials -> creative tagging, audience preference analysis, conversion diagnosis, placement-strategy feedback.
- Printing and routing contracts -> contract structure, client-supplier relationships, revenue and procurement chain awareness.
- Manually configuring hundreds of coupons -> dependency mapping, error analysis, product requirement draft, automation opportunity.
- Downloading every competitor app -> competitor matrix, campaign-pattern library, differentiated proposal.
